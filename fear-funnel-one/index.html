The Risks In Your Home Right Now

Most Homeowners Don't Know What's Building Inside Their Property

Our IICRC-certified inspectors use thermal imaging and multi-point risk assessment to detect hidden vulnerabilities — moisture, electrical heat signatures, structural stress points — that are invisible to the naked eye until they've caused catastrophic damage.

Supply Line Failures

Braided supply lines under sinks and toilets fail silently, often releasing hundreds of gallons before anyone notices. Most are never inspected.

Avg. repair cost: $3,000–$8,000

Hidden Slab Leaks

Underground pipe leaks go undetected for months beneath concrete slabs. By the time your floors buckle or your water bill spikes, significant damage is already done.

Avg. repair cost: $5,000–$15,000

Water Heater Failure

The average water heater fails between years 8 and 12. Most homeowners don't know their unit's age. A catastrophic failure can release 40–80 gallons instantly.

Avg. damage cost: $4,000–$9,000

Attic Moisture Buildup

Poor ventilation causes condensation to accumulate in attic spaces, silently spreading into insulation and roof decking before visible signs appear.

Avg. repair cost: $2,000–$7,000

Appliance Connections

Washing machine hoses are the #1 cause of preventable interior flooding. Dishwasher and refrigerator connections are rarely checked and harden over time.

Avg. damage cost: $3,500–$10,000

Freeze Events

North Texas saw thousands of burst pipes in 2021 alone. Uninsulated pipes in exterior walls and attics are catastrophic failures waiting for the next hard freeze.

Avg. damage cost: $8,000–$25,000

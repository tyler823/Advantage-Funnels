The Real Business Risk

What Happens to Your Business While You Wait for Repairs?

For residential properties, water damage is traumatic but survivable. For commercial properties, every hour your doors are closed is revenue you can never recover. Here's what each business type faces.

Medical Offices & Clinics

A ceiling leak or pipe failure can contaminate exam rooms, destroy records, trigger HIPAA compliance reviews, and force patient rescheduling across weeks — not days.

Average closure cost: $15,000–$40,000 in lost appointments + remediation

Multifamily / Apartments

A single burst pipe can simultaneously damage multiple units, displace residents, trigger liability exposure, and generate months of negative reviews on every rental platform.

A 12-unit displacement event can cost $60,000+ in remediation + lost rent

Retail & Restaurants

Retail closures mean zero revenue. For restaurants, a water event means failed health inspections, inventory loss, equipment damage, and staff disruption — all at once.

Average 3-day closure: $12,000–$25,000 in direct lost revenue

Hotels & Hospitality

Water damage on any floor can cascade to rooms below, take entire wings offline, and generate refund obligations and reputation damage through review platforms instantly.

40-room offline event: $30,000–$80,000 in lost room revenue + repairs

Industrial & Warehouse

Equipment, inventory, and flooring damage from water events can halt production lines for days or weeks. Supply chain disruption compounds the damage far beyond the physical repairs.

Production line stoppage: $50,000–$200,000+ in lost output

Office Buildings

Tenant relations, lease obligations, and liability exposure all converge when a water event forces evacuation. Tenant retention is directly tied to how your building responds.

Tenant departure risk + legal exposure: difficult to quantify but significant
